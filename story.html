Idea

The eye of the creator was attracted by an ordinary river pebble. It was thought that its sharp edges eventually wear down the current, rubbing stone against stone, until it acquires a simple and perfect form. And then Rubik decided to properly "grind" his cubes. Naturally, I had to discard everything superfluous. As it is easy to calculate, 26 small cubes have a total of 156 faces. Of these, Rubik boldly cut off more than a hundred and left only 54 outer faces: one-color for six central cubes, two-color for twelve side ones, and three-color for eight corner ones.

Erno Rubik

Erno Rubik, at that moment still an ordinary teacher, works at the Academy of Applied Arts in Budapest. Having made a cube model as a visual aid for teaching students, he realizes that he has invented a rather serious and interesting puzzle and a year later, in 1975, he receives a patent for it. The first batches of the cube were released in Hungary and sold in a toy store, due to which, most likely, not many people tried to deal with it seriously. Later, the puzzle spread to other Western countries, France, England, and the USA. Their debut took place, as well as in Hungary, at toy fairs, thanks to which the cube received the status of the same name there.
